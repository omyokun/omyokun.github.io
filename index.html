About Me

Welcome! I am a Ph.D. student in the Computer Science Department at the University of Toulouse, France. I am supervised by Nicholas Asher. My research focuses on understanding the limits of Large Language Models, with a particular emphasis on in-context learning (ICL). Based on these findings, I have also worked on multi-agent systems and on efficient pruning techniques for LLMs used in agentic settings. I maintain a strong interest in core Machine Learning and Mathematics.

Prior to joining the University of Toulouse, I completed the Classes Préparatoires at Lycée Mohammed VI d’Excellence in Benguérir (CPGE MPSI/MP*). I then joined ENSEEIHT in Toulouse, where I obtained an engineering degree in Computer Science with a specialization in High-Performance Computing and Big Data. During my engineering studies, I developed strong foundations in optimization, machine learning, statistics, numerical methods, and large-scale data processing.
